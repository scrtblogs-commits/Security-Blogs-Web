# Indirect Competitors — General SEO Agencies, Freelancers & DIY Platforms

> **Scope:** Entities that are not security-specialist but compete for the same marketing budget from security industry prospects. Includes large Australian SEO agencies, freelance consultants, and DIY SEO/marketing platforms.

---

## Why Indirect Competitors Matter

Security industry prospects frequently evaluate SecurityBlogs against:
1. Large full-service SEO agencies they've heard of through advertising
2. Cheaper freelance alternatives found via Upwork or Google
3. DIY platforms they already pay subscriptions for (Semrush, Ahrefs)
4. Their web developer who "does SEO on the side"

Understanding these alternatives allows SecurityBlogs to position effectively at every sales conversation touchpoint.

## Category B: Freelance SEO Consultants

### 6. Independent SEO Consultants (Upwork / Local)
- **Platform:** Upwork, LinkedIn, Local referral
- **Pricing:** $50–$200/hr or $500–$2,500/mo retainers
- **Typical Profile:** 1-2 person operation, generalist SEO skills
- **Strengths:**
  - Lower cost
  - Direct access to the consultant
  - Flexible engagement terms
- **Weaknesses vs SecurityBlogs:**
  - No security industry knowledge
  - No content creation capacity for technical security topics
  - No owned media or link building assets in the niche
  - No team for scale — one person bottleneck
  - No AEO, GEO, or AI Visibility capability
  - No advertising inventory or newsletter reach
- **How to Beat Them:** "A freelancer can optimise your page titles. We can get your brand appearing in AI search results, place articles on our DA 68 publication, and send your content to 24,000 security professionals."
- **Prospect Signal:** Clients evaluating freelancers are typically price-sensitive SMEs — qualify carefully, emphasise ROI over service volume.

---

### 7. Web Developers Offering SEO Add-Ons
- **Platform:** Local web studios, WooCommerce/WordPress developers
- **Pricing:** Bundled with web builds or $300–$800/mo
- **Typical Profile:** Developer who sets up Yoast and calls it SEO
- **Strengths:**
  - Existing relationship (built the website)
  - Low switching cost for client
  - Single vendor simplicity
- **Weaknesses vs SecurityBlogs:**
  - SEO as afterthought, not core competency
  - No content strategy, keyword research depth, or link building
  - No security industry knowledge
  - Technical SEO often misconfigured
- **How to Beat Them:** Education play — offer a free SEO audit that reveals gaps their developer has left. Show the missed opportunity in concrete terms.

## Category C: DIY SEO Platforms

### 8. Semrush (DIY)
- **Type:** SaaS SEO platform
- **Pricing:** $129–$499+/mo
- **Typical User:** Marketing managers, business owners attempting in-house SEO
- **Strengths:**
  - Powerful keyword and competitor research tools
  - Comprehensive site audit capability
  - Content marketing workflow features
  - Brand recognition — security prospects may already have subscriptions
- **Weaknesses vs SecurityBlogs:**
  - Tool only — no execution, no strategy, no content creation
  - Requires significant SEO knowledge to use effectively
  - No security industry context built in
  - No link building, guest posting, or AI Visibility capability
  - Time cost on security business owner is significant
- **Objection to Handle:** "We already use Semrush." Response: "Semrush tells you what to do — we do it for you, with security industry expertise built in. The tool is only as good as the person using it."
