## Section 3: Content Formats Competitors Use Well (That SecurityBlogs Should Adopt or Improve)

### 3.1 Video Content

**Competitors with strong video:**
- **Security Info Watch** — CEO video interviews, product demos, webinar recordings
- **IFSEC Global** — trade show floor interviews, product previews, virtual summit recordings
- **Hikvision AU / Dahua AU** — product demonstration YouTube channels

**SecurityBlogs Gap:** No dedicated YouTube channel strategy; video content is absent from editorial output.

**Opportunity:**
- Launch SecurityBlogs YouTube channel with: security product explainers, "Security Industry 101" educational series, interviews with AU security professionals and executives, "Ask the Expert" Q&A shorts
- Video content is increasingly cited in AI answers; YouTube videos appear in Google AI Overviews
- Video transcripts create additional indexable text content on-page

**Estimated Impact:** High — video is the fastest-growing content format for B2B audiences; YouTube is the second-largest search engine; video embeds increase time-on-page and reduce bounce rates

## Section 5: Content Format Recommendations by Priority

### Formats to Adopt Immediately (Quick Wins — Low Effort, High Return)

1. **FAQ-optimised service pages** — Every SecurityBlogs service (SEO, AEO, GEO, Google Ads, Web Design, Guest Posting) needs a long-form FAQ-enriched landing page optimised for AI citation
2. **"Complete Guide" pillar articles** — One comprehensive guide per major service area and topic cluster; these become the foundation for AI citations and organic rankings
3. **Comparison articles** — "X vs Y" format for security companies, products, and services; high commercial intent, frequently AI-retrieved
4. **How-to guides** — Process-based content for both security industry business owners and security buyers; HowTo schema marks them for AI extraction

### Formats to Build Over 3–6 Months (Major Projects — High Return, Requires Resource)

1. **Original research / annual reports** — Salary surveys, market data, technology adoption surveys; highest-authority content format
2. **Video series on YouTube** — Security industry education, product explainers, expert interviews
3. **Podcast** — Security industry conversation; guest appearances by AU security leaders
4. **Interactive tools** — Cost calculators, self-assessment tools, budget planners

### Formats to Develop Over 6–12 Months (Long-Term Authority)

1. **Accredited training or resource library** — CPD-eligible content for security professionals; subscription or gated access model
2. **Security industry database** — Licensed security companies, approved products, installer directory — structured data that AI can retrieve for directory queries
3. **Webinar series** — Regular live educational events for security professionals; recordings become indexed video content
4. **Print supplement / annual guide** — SEN-style print content for trade show distribution at SecurityEx and ASIAL events
