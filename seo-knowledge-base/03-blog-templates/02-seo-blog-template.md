# [H1: PRIMARY KEYWORD — Written as a Compelling Blog Post Title]

ℹ️ *Section Note — H1:* Must include the primary keyword. Should read like a genuine article headline — not a dry keyword phrase. Use numbers, years, questions, or "how to" framing to increase CTR. The H1 can differ slightly from the meta title.

Examples of strong H1s:
- "How Much Do Security Guards Cost in Australia? (2025 Pricing Guide)"
- "The Complete Guide to CCTV Installation for Australian Businesses"
- "7 Things to Check Before Hiring a Security Company in Australia"

**Published:** [DATE — e.g. 12 June 2025] | **Updated:** [DATE IF UPDATED] | **By:** [AUTHOR NAME], [AUTHOR TITLE]  
**Reading time:** [e.g. 8 minutes]

---

### INTRO PARAGRAPH

[INTRO PARAGRAPH — 100–150 words structured as: HOOK → PROBLEM → PROMISE]

ℹ️ *Section Note — Intro Paragraph:* The intro must do three things in this order:
1. **Hook** — Open with a surprising stat, bold claim, relatable scenario, or direct question. Do NOT start with "In today's world..." or "Security is important because...". Grab attention in the first sentence.
2. **Problem** — Acknowledge the reader's pain point or knowledge gap in 2–3 sentences. Show them you understand their situation.
3. **Promise** — Tell them exactly what they'll learn by reading this post. Be specific. Example: "In this guide, we cover the average hourly rates for security guards in every Australian state, the factors that drive costs up or down, and how to benchmark quotes before you sign a contract."

The primary keyword must appear in the first 100 words of the post.

Example intro:
> "Hiring a security guard in Australia without knowing the market rate is like buying a car without knowing the price list. You'll likely overpay — and not realise it until it's too late. Security guard costs vary significantly across Australia depending on state, licence class, shift type, and contract length. This guide breaks down what you should expect to pay in 2025, what drives pricing up or down, and how to ensure you're getting a competitive rate."

## PART 3 — INTERNAL LINK SUGGESTIONS

ℹ️ *Section Note — Internal Links:* Insert these links naturally within body copy — not as a separate block at the bottom of the post. Aim for 3–5 internal links per post. Use anchor text that describes the destination page's topic — not generic phrases like "click here" or "read more."

| Destination Page | Suggested Anchor Text | Placement in Post |
|------------------|-----------------------|-------------------|
| `/services/[service-slug]/` | "[SERVICE NAME] for Australian businesses" | Section 1 or 2 |
| `/blog/[related-post-slug]/` | "[RELATED POST TOPIC]" | Section 3 or 4 |
| `/directory/` | "SecurityBlogs provider directory" | Section 4 or conclusion |
| `/guides/[guide-slug]/` | "[GUIDE TOPIC] guide" | Section 2 or conclusion |
| `/contact/` | "get a free quote" or "speak to our team" | Conclusion CTA |
