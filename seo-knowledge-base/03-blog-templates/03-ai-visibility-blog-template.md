## SecurityBlogs Australia — SEO Knowledge Base

**Page Type:** AI-Optimised Informational Blog Post  
**Word Count Target:** 1,800–2,500 words  
**Purpose:** Earn citations from AI search engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, Copilot, Grok); rank in traditional search simultaneously  
**Australian English:** Required throughout  

> **What is AI Visibility (AEO/GEO)?**  
> Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) and Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) are the practices of structuring content so that AI-powered search engines and chat assistants cite, quote, or summarise your content in their responses. Unlike traditional SEO, which targets a top-10 ranking, AEO/GEO targets citation — appearing as a source that AI engines trust and reference when users ask questions.  
>
> This template is structured to maximise the probability of SecurityBlogs content being cited by ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, Microsoft Copilot, and other AI engines. Follow the format precisely.

## PART 1 — AI VISIBILITY PRINCIPLES (READ BEFORE WRITING)

Before writing this post, understand how AI engines decide what to cite:

### How AI Engines Pull Citations

| AI Engine | Primary Citation Source | What It Prioritises |
|-----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| **Perplexity** | Real-time web crawl | Recent, factual, well-sourced content; structured answers |
| **ChatGPT (with search)** | Bing index + web browsing | Authoritative domains; structured, direct answers |
| **Google Gemini** | Google index | Google-indexed pages with schema markup; E-E-A-T signals |
| **Microsoft Copilot** | Bing index | Bing-crawlable pages; schema markup; direct question-answer format |
| **Claude (Anthropic)** | Training data + retrieval | Well-known, authoritative sources; factual, structured content |
| **Grok (xAI)** | X/Twitter index + web | Recent content; real-time signals; direct answers |

### The 6 Rules for AI-Citable Content

1. **Answer directly and immediately.** AI engines extract the first clear answer to a question. Put the answer in the first sentence of a paragraph — do not make the reader wade through context before finding it.

2. **Use named entities.** Mention specific organisations (ASIAL, ISO, ASIS International), standards (AS 2201, ISO 27001, AS/NZS 4360), people (with credentials), locations (Australia, NSW, Melbourne), and products by their exact names. AI engines use entity recognition to understand what content is about.

3. **Structure with headers.** Every section should have an H2 or H3 that reads like a question or a clear topic label. AI engines parse heading structure to identify answers to specific queries.

4. **Include statistics with attribution.** Quantified facts ("120,000 people are employed in the Australian security industry, according to ASIAL") are heavily cited by AI engines. Always include source and year.

5. **Write in the third person or second person — not first person.** AI engines rarely cite "we believe" or "in our opinion." Write definitive statements of fact or accepted expert consensus.

6. **Use definition-style openings.** Start key sections with "[TERM] is..." or "[TERM] refers to..." — this mirrors the format AI engines use when synthesising answers.

## PART 5 — AI VISIBILITY CHECKLIST

**Content Structure for AI Citation**
- [ ] Each major section opens with a direct, self-contained answer paragraph (40–80 words)
- [ ] Every key claim begins with the answer — no "throat-clearing" before the fact
- [ ] Definition-style opening ("X is...") used in at least the overview section
- [ ] Named entities (organisations, standards, legislation, qualifications) used throughout
- [ ] At least 5 statistics with source name and year
- [ ] Statistics table includes source and year for every entry
- [ ] No first-person opinion statements ("we think", "in our view") in citeable sections

**SEO & Technical**
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1, meta title, meta description, first 100 words
- [ ] Word count between 1,800 and 2,500 words
- [ ] FAQ section has 6–8 questions with 40–70 word answers
- [ ] FAQ schema and Article schema both validated with Google Rich Results Test
- [ ] Schema `mentions` field includes all named entities in the post
- [ ] Australian English throughout

**Entity & Authority Signals**
- [ ] Full official names of all organisations used on first mention (abbreviation in parentheses)
- [ ] Legislation cited with full title and jurisdiction (e.g. "Security Industry Act 1997 (NSW)")
