# 05 — Comparison Page Template
## SecurityBlogs Australia — SEO Knowledge Base

**Page Types:** "X vs Y" comparisons, "Best [X] for Security Companies", "[Service A] vs [Service B]", Top lists  
**Word Count Target:** 2,000–3,000 words  
**Purpose:** Capture commercial-investigation-intent keywords; rank for "vs" and "best" queries; drive traffic from buyers actively evaluating options  
**Australian English:** Required throughout  

> **Why comparison pages are powerful:** Queries like "X vs Y" and "best [X] for [audience]" signal that the searcher is close to a purchasing decision. They've moved past the awareness stage — they know what category of solution they need and are now evaluating specific options. Comparison pages that directly answer this intent with clear, unbiased analysis and a definitive recommendation convert at significantly higher rates than generic informational content.

---

## PART 1 — HOW TO RANK COMPARISON PAGES (READ BEFORE WRITING)

### Search Intent for Comparison Pages

Comparison page visitors have **commercial investigation intent** — they are researching before buying. Google and AI engines recognise this intent and expect content that:

1. **Directly answers the comparison** — Do not make the reader wade through 500 words of preamble before reaching the actual comparison. State the key difference clearly in the intro.

2. **Uses structured data (tables)** — Comparison tables are extracted by Google for featured snippets and by AI engines for direct answers. Always include a summary comparison table.

3. **Declares a clear winner or recommendation** — "It depends" is the least useful answer a comparison page can give. Commit to a recommendation (with appropriate caveats). Readers searching "X vs Y" want a verdict.

4. **Covers both options thoroughly and fairly** — One-sided comparisons signal bias and reduce trust. Give genuine pros and cons for each option, even when recommending one over the other.

5. **Targets the exact "vs" keyword phrase** — The H1, meta title, and URL slug should contain the exact search query (e.g. "CCTV vs security guards" or "security guard cost vs alarm system cost").

6. **Answers follow-up questions in FAQs** — After the main comparison, users often have follow-up questions. A comprehensive FAQ section captures these and increases dwell time.
