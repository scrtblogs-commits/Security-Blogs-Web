# 04 — Security Industry Editorial Blog Template
## SecurityBlogs Australia — SEO Knowledge Base

**Page Type:** Security Industry Editorial — News, Analysis, or Practical Guide  
**Word Count Target:** 1,200–1,800 words  
**Audience:** Australian security industry professionals, business owners procuring security services, compliance officers, facility managers  
**Tone:** Authoritative, practical, Australian — professional without being academic  
**Australian English:** Required throughout  

> **About this template:** SecurityBlogs publishes two types of editorial content — (1) **news and analysis** covering developments in the Australian security industry, and (2) **practical guides** that help security buyers and professionals navigate specific challenges. This template supports both formats. The layout notes below indicate which sections are mandatory and which are optional depending on your format choice.  
>
> Choose your format at the top of the article brief:  
> - `[FORMAT: NEWS/ANALYSIS]` — for timely industry developments, regulatory changes, incident analysis  
> - `[FORMAT: PRACTICAL GUIDE]` — for how-to content, buyer's guides, compliance walkthroughs

# [H1: ARTICLE HEADLINE — Clear, Specific, Journalistic or Guide-Style]

ℹ️ *Section Note — H1:* For news/analysis: write it like a news headline — specific, active voice, and immediately informative. For guides: include the primary keyword and state the outcome. Both formats should make the reader immediately understand what the article covers without reading further. Avoid clickbait framing — the SecurityBlogs audience is industry-sophisticated.

News examples:
- "Queensland Expands Security Licence Categories Following Industry Consultation"
- "What the 2025 Work Health and Safety Amendments Mean for Security Operators"

Guide examples:
- "How Australian Businesses Can Comply with AS 2201 Intruder Alarm Standards"

## [H2: CONTEXT HEADLINE — Sets the Scene for the Reader]

ℹ️ *Section Note — Industry Context:*  
This section grounds the article in the real Australian security landscape. It should answer: "Why does this topic matter right now?" For news pieces, reference the specific event, regulation, incident, or announcement that prompted the article. For guides, describe the current challenge or environment that makes this guidance necessary.

Mandatory for both formats. Length: 150–250 words.

Include where relevant:
- Current state of the relevant sector in Australia
- Recent developments or drivers (regulatory, technology, market)
- Reference to Australian bodies, standards, or legislation by full official name
- A statistic or data point with source and year

### SECTION 2: THE PROBLEM OR CHALLENGE

## [H2: WHAT THE CHALLENGE IS — State It Plainly]

ℹ️ *Section Note — Problem Section:*  
This section names the specific problem, gap, risk, or challenge the article addresses. It should be written from the reader's perspective — not from the perspective of the industry in the abstract. What do security professionals, business owners, or compliance officers actually face?

For news: What is the problem that this development responds to, or what new problem does it create?  
For guides: What is the operational or compliance challenge the guide helps readers solve?

Length: 200–300 words. Use subheadings (H3) if covering multiple dimensions of the problem.

[PARAGRAPH 1 — 80–120 words. Name the problem directly. Avoid hedging or over-qualifying. Example: "The core challenge for Australian businesses is that security licensing requirements vary by state, creating compliance complexity for national operators and confusion for buyers who engage security companies across multiple jurisdictions."]

[PARAGRAPH 2 — 80–120 words. Quantify or contextualise the problem where possible. What are the consequences of not addressing it? Reference real cases, regulatory consequences, or cost impacts where available.]

### SECTION 4: EXPERT PERSPECTIVE

## Expert View: [TOPIC OR DEBATE]

ℹ️ *Section Note — Expert Perspective:*  
Including an expert voice increases E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) signals and differentiates SecurityBlogs content from generic AI-written material. The expert should be a named, credentialled Australian security professional — an ASIAL member, a licensed consultant, a regulator, or an academic. Obtain written permission or source from a published interview, media release, or publicly attributed statement. Do not fabricate quotes.

If no expert quote is available for a given article, replace this section with a "Industry Perspective" box summarising the prevailing view among practitioners (clearly attributed as editorial synthesis, not a direct quote).
