### Pillar Page Outline Summary

This pillar addresses the emerging discipline of AI Visibility — getting security brands cited, recommended and referenced by large language models (LLMs) and AI search platforms. Unlike GEO (which focuses on search-based AI like Google AI Overviews), AI Visibility targets a broader set of AI platforms including ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude and others. For security companies, this is about ensuring that when a prospect asks an AI assistant "recommend a security company in Sydney," your brand appears.

## AI Visibility Notes & Considerations

- **Most rapidly evolving cluster:** AI platform capabilities change faster than SEO. All content should be date-stamped with "verified as of [month/year]" notices.
- **Measurement is the biggest challenge:** Unlike SEO, there are no standardised analytics integrations for AI visibility. SecurityBlogs should develop its own methodology and publish it — this becomes a credibility-building content piece in itself.
- **Security industry opportunity:** Security companies are under-represented in AI knowledge bases compared to sectors like finance, health and law. First-mover advantage for Australian security brands is significant.
- **Do not conflate GEO and AI Visibility:** GEO is specifically about search-engine-based AI (Google AI Overviews, Bing AI). AI Visibility is broader and includes LLM chatbots not integrated into search engines. Both are important but require different tactics.
- **Privacy and operational security:** When discussing AI citing security company information, note that some operational details (patrol routes, client lists, security system configurations) should not be publicly indexed. This is a relevant differentiating point to raise in the content.
- **Australian English throughout:** Use "optimise", "recognise", "organise", "licence" (noun), "practise" (verb).
