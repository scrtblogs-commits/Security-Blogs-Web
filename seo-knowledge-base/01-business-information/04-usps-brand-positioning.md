## Tone of Voice Guide

### The SecurityBlogs Voice Is:
- **Authoritative** — We know this industry. Statements are confident and backed by data or editorial experience.
- **Direct** — Get to the point. Buyers are busy professionals; they don't have time for preamble.
- **Data-driven** — Use numbers where they exist. "180,000+ monthly readers" not "a large audience."
- **Precise** — Name the thing. "Gallagher access control" not "access control systems." "ASIAL" not "the industry body."
- **Credible** — No hype. No superlatives without evidence. Claims are grounded.

### The SecurityBlogs Voice Is NOT:
- Casual or colloquial (not "hey there!" or "awesome")
- Vague or generic ("we help businesses grow" — meaningless)
- Jargon-heavy without explanation (define acronyms on first use)
- Overpromising ("guaranteed first page rankings" — never)
- American in spelling or idiom

## Australian English Style Guide

SecurityBlogs uses Australian English throughout all content. The following conventions apply:

### Spelling
- Organisation (not organization)
- Recognise, optimise, authorise (not -ize)
- Licence (noun) / license (verb)
- Programme (where referring to a plan or schedule); program (software)
- Centre (not center)
- Colour, behaviour, neighbour (not -or)
- Travelling, modelling (double l)
- Cheque (not check, in financial context)

### Date and Number Format
- Dates: DD Month YYYY (e.g., 15 June 2026) — not US format
- Currency: AUD or $ with Australian context clear; use $X,XXX format
- Phone: Australian format — (03) XXXX XXXX or 04XX XXX XXX

### Terminology
- "Mobile" not "cell phone"
- "Footpath" not "sidewalk"
- "Carpark" not "parking lot"
- "Financial year" not "fiscal year"
- "Tender" not "bid" (for government procurement)
- Reference ASIAL, not generic "the industry association"

### Punctuation
- Single quotation marks for scare quotes: 'security expert'
- Full stops inside quotation marks: He said "we need better access control."
- Oxford comma: optional but consistent within a document
