# Meta Title Rules — SecurityBlogs Standard

## Purpose

Meta titles (title tags) are the single most important on-page SEO element. They are the headline that appears in Google search results, browser tabs, and social share previews. Every page on SecurityBlogs and every client site must follow these rules.

---

## Character Limits

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum length | 60 characters (including spaces) |
| Minimum length | 40 characters |
| Ideal range | 50–60 characters |

Google truncates titles beyond approximately 600px in width (~60 characters). Titles shorter than 40 characters waste ranking opportunity.

---

## Format Rules

### Standard Format
`[Primary Keyword] | [Brand Name]`

**Example:** `Security Camera Installation Sydney | SecurityBlogs`

### For Blog Posts
`[Post Title Including Primary Keyword] | SecurityBlogs`

**Example:** `How to Choose an Access Control System for Your Office | SecurityBlogs`

### For Service Pages
`[Service] for [Industry/Location] | SecurityBlogs`

**Example:** `Security SEO Services for CCTV & Access Control Companies | SecurityBlogs`

### For Location Pages
`[Service] [City/Suburb] | [Brand Name]`

**Example:** `Security Alarm Installation Brisbane | SecurityBlogs`

### For Knowledge Hub / Blog
`[Engaging Title with Keyword] | SecurityBlogs`

**Example:** `AEO vs AIO: What's the Difference for Security Businesses? | SecurityBlogs`

---

## Keyword Rules

1. **Primary keyword must appear in the title** — always
2. **Primary keyword should be at the start of the title** — not buried at the end
3. **Do not repeat the same keyword twice** — keyword stuffing is penalised
4. **Do not use ALL CAPS** — looks spammy
5. **Do not use special characters** (!, @, #, $, %) except for pipes (|) as separators
6. **Each page must have a unique title** — no duplicate title tags across the site

---

## Brand Suffix

All SecurityBlogs pages end with `| SecurityBlogs`

The pipe ( | ) is the preferred separator. Do not use a dash (–), colon (:), or hyphen (-) as the separator.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---|---|
| `SecurityBlogs - Best SEO for Security` | `Security SEO for CCTV & Access Control Companies | SecurityBlogs` |
| `Home | SecurityBlogs` | `Security Marketing Agency for the Security Industry | SecurityBlogs` |
| `Blog Post Title` (no brand) | `Blog Post Title | SecurityBlogs` |
| `Security SEO Security SEO Services` | `Security SEO Services | SecurityBlogs` |
| `WE OFFER THE BEST SECURITY SEO!!!` | `Security SEO Services for Australian Security Businesses | SecurityBlogs` |
| 78 character title that gets truncated... | 52 character title fits perfectly |

---

## Title Writing Tips for Security Content

- Include the security product or service type when possible (CCTV, access control, alarm)
- Include location for local SEO pages (city or state)
- Include intent modifier for commercial/transactional pages (service, installer, company, cost)
- Include question format for informational content (How to, What is, Why does)
- Include year for freshness signals on trend/news content (2025, 2026)

---

## Title Tag Checklist

Before publishing any page, verify:
- [ ] Title is between 50–60 characters
- [ ] Primary keyword appears in the title, ideally at the start
- [ ] Title ends with `| SecurityBlogs`
- [ ] Title is unique (no other page has the same title)
- [ ] No keyword stuffing
- [ ] Written in Australian English
- [ ] Accurately describes the page content
