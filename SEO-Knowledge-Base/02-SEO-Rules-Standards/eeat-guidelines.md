# E-E-A-T Guidelines — SecurityBlogs Standard

## What Is E-E-A-T?

E-E-A-T stands for **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness**. It is Google's framework for evaluating content quality, particularly for topics that can significantly affect people's lives (health, finance, legal, safety — known as "Your Money or Your Life" / YMYL topics).

Security content — particularly content about security systems, cybersecurity, and business safety — has YMYL characteristics. Google applies stricter E-E-A-T standards to security content.

**E-E-A-T is not a direct ranking factor** — it is a framework used by Google's Search Quality Evaluators to assess content quality, which in turn informs algorithm signals.

### How SecurityBlogs Demonstrates Experience

- **Client case studies** with real outcomes (not hypothetical) — 50+ security brand engagements documented
- **Real data** from security client campaigns (+180% traffic, 3.2× ROAS, 87% AI citation rate)
- **Industry-specific knowledge** evidenced by correct use of security terminology, regulations, and technology
- **Original research** — SecurityBlogs conducts its own industry research and publishes findings
- **Tools built from experience** — the AI Visibility Score tool, the local pack checker, and the AI Visibility Challenge are all built on real observation of the security SEO landscape

### Content Rules for Experience Signals
- Include specific, verifiable examples (client type, outcome, timeframe) — even without naming clients publicly
- Reference real tools, platforms, and processes used in actual work
- Acknowledge limitations and nuances — experienced practitioners know what doesn't always work
- Use first-person perspective ("In our work with security installers in Sydney...") where appropriate

## Trustworthiness

**What it means:** The website and its content can be trusted — it is safe, honest, and transparent.

### How SecurityBlogs Demonstrates Trustworthiness

- **Real company details** — physical country (Australia), phone number (+61 411 212 418), email (info@securityblogs.com.au)
- **Transparent pricing** — pricing guidelines published on the website
- **Clear terms and policies** — Privacy Policy, Terms of Service accessible from footer
- **HTTPS** — SSL certificate active on all pages
- **No deceptive practices** — no false claims, no hidden fees, no misleading statistics
- **Contact accessible** — contact page with multiple methods to reach the team
- **About Us page** — names the founder, explains the company's background and mission
- **Case studies with real outcomes** — no inflated or fabricated results

### Content Rules for Trustworthiness
- Never make claims that cannot be substantiated with evidence
- Clearly label opinions as opinions, not facts
- Include date of publication and date of last update on all blog posts
- Link to sources when citing statistics or research
- Disclose any sponsored content or affiliate relationships
- Respond promptly to contact form enquiries and comments

## E-E-A-T Checklist

For every piece of content published on SecurityBlogs:
- [ ] Author name and bio are visible
- [ ] Author bio links to a full author profile page
- [ ] Publication date is shown
- [ ] "Last updated" date shown (for evergreen content updated over time)
- [ ] Sources are cited for statistics and research claims
- [ ] Content is technically accurate and up to date
- [ ] Content includes at least one real example or case reference
- [ ] No unsubstantiated superlatives ("best," "most powerful," "only") without evidence
- [ ] Company contact details visible on or linked from the page
