# Internal Linking Rules — SecurityBlogs Standard

## Purpose

Internal links serve three critical SEO functions:
1. **PageRank distribution** — pass authority from high-authority pages to priority pages
2. **Crawlability** — help Google discover and index all pages on the site
3. **User experience** — guide readers to relevant content and deeper in the funnel

A strong internal linking structure is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-cost SEO activities available.

---

## Core Internal Linking Rules

### Rule 1: Every Page Must Have Internal Links TO It
No "orphan pages" — every page on SecurityBlogs must be linked to from at least one other page. Orphan pages do not accumulate PageRank and are crawled less frequently.

### Rule 2: Every Page Must Have Internal Links FROM It
Every page should link outward to relevant supporting content, service pages, or related resources. A minimum of 2–3 internal links per page.

### Rule 3: Use Descriptive Anchor Text
Anchor text must describe the destination page's content. Never use generic anchor text.

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---|---|
| Click here | our Security SEO service |
| Read more | how to optimise for Google's AI Overviews |
| This page | the SecurityBlogs AEO guide |
| Learn more | access control SEO case study |

### Rule 4: Match Anchor Text to Target Page's Primary Keyword
When linking to a page, use anchor text that matches or closely resembles that page's target keyword. This reinforces the page's topical relevance signal.

### Rule 5: Link Contextually (Within Body Content)
Contextual links (links within body paragraphs) are more valuable than navigation links or footer links. Always look for opportunities to link within the content body.

### Rule 6: Avoid Over-Optimisation
Do not use the exact same anchor text every time you link to the same page. Vary the anchor text naturally:
- Exact keyword anchor: "Security SEO services"
- Partial keyword anchor: "security SEO"
- Branded anchor: "SecurityBlogs Security SEO"
- Natural language anchor: "our specialist SEO for security companies"

### Rule 7: Link Deep, Not Just to the Homepage
The homepage and top-level service pages already have the most authority. Focus internal links on directing authority to:
- Specific service sub-pages
- High-value blog posts in the Knowledge Hub
- Location pages
- Case studies

## Internal Linking Architecture

### Tier 1: Homepage → Tier 2: Service Pages
The homepage links to all 7 service pages (SEO, AIO, AEO, GEO, Google Ads, Bing Ads, Web Design) and to the Knowledge Hub.

### Tier 2: Service Pages → Tier 3: Supporting Content
Each service page links to:
- Related blog posts in the Knowledge Hub
- Relevant case studies
- Related service pages (cross-linking between complementary services)
- Free tools (AI Visibility Score, AI Visibility Challenge)

### Tier 3: Blog Posts → Tier 2: Service Pages
Every blog post should link to at least one relevant service page. This is how authority from content flows back up to the commercial pages.

### Tier 3: Blog Posts → Other Blog Posts
Blog posts should link to related posts within the same topic cluster and to posts in adjacent clusters.

## Internal Linking for the Knowledge Hub

The Knowledge Hub is the primary content authority engine for SecurityBlogs. Internal linking within the Knowledge Hub follows a topic cluster model:

### Pillar Page → Supporting Pages
Each pillar page (main topic overview) links to all supporting pages (sub-topic deep dives) within that cluster.

### Supporting Pages → Pillar Page
Every supporting blog post links back to its pillar page. This concentrates topical authority on the pillar.

### Supporting Pages → Other Supporting Pages
Related posts within a cluster cross-link to each other.

## Minimum Internal Link Requirements

| Page Type | Minimum Outbound Internal Links | Required Destinations |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage | 8+ | All service pages, Knowledge Hub, contact |
| Service page | 4+ | Related service, 2 blog posts, contact/CTA |
| Blog post | 3+ | Relevant service page, pillar page, 1 related post |
| Case study | 3+ | Relevant service page, related case study, contact |
| Location page | 3+ | Main service page, related location page, contact |
| Knowledge Hub category | 4+ | All posts in category, related service, pillar |

---

## Internal Linking Tools & Process

1. **When writing new content:** Identify 3–5 existing pages to link to before you start writing
2. **When adding to the Knowledge Hub:** Always link to the relevant service page and pillar post
3. **Monthly audit:** Review recently published pages and identify opportunities to add internal links from older pages to new content
4. **Silo review:** Quarterly review of internal link structure to ensure no orphan pages and authority is flowing to priority commercial pages

---

## Internal Linking Checklist

Before publishing any page, verify:
- [ ] Page links to at least 3 internal pages
- [ ] Page links to at least one relevant service page
- [ ] Anchor text is descriptive (no "click here" or "read more")
- [ ] Page is linked to from at least one other existing page
- [ ] Links open in the same tab (not new tab) for internal links
- [ ] No broken internal links
- [ ] Anchor text is varied (not identical on every link to the same page)
