# Content Quality Standards — SecurityBlogs Standard

## Purpose

These standards define the minimum quality bar for all content published on SecurityBlogs. Every blog post, service page, case study, and knowledge hub article must meet these standards before publication.

---

## Content Quality Principles

### 1. Original
No content on SecurityBlogs is copied, spun, or substantially paraphrased from other sources. Every piece must contain original thinking, original analysis, original examples, or original data that cannot be found elsewhere.

**Test:** Could a competitor copy-paste this content onto their site without editing? If yes, it's not original enough.

### 2. Specific
Generic advice is not acceptable. SecurityBlogs content must be specific to:
- The security industry (not generic "small business" advice with "security" added)
- The specific topic (not surface-level overviews that cover nothing in depth)
- The specific audience (security installers vs. SaaS vendors vs. manufacturers — different advice for different segments)

**Test:** Could this content apply equally well to a plumber or a restaurant? If yes, it's not specific enough.

### 3. Accurate
All factual claims must be correct and current. Outdated information about Google algorithms, AI systems, security regulations, or industry data must be updated promptly.

**Test:** Has this been fact-checked against primary sources (Google documentation, ASIAL publications, peer-reviewed research)?

### 4. Useful
Content must solve a real problem or answer a genuine question that the target reader has. "Useful" is defined from the reader's perspective, not the writer's.

**Test:** After reading this, can the reader do something they couldn't do before, or understand something they didn't understand before?

### 5. Complete
Content covers the topic comprehensively enough that the reader does not need to go elsewhere to complete their understanding. This does not mean exhaustive length — it means appropriate depth for the topic.

**Test:** Are there obvious questions a reader would have after finishing this content that aren't answered?

## Minimum Content Length Guidelines

| Content Type | Minimum Word Count | Target Word Count |
|---|---|---|
| Blog post (general) | 1,000 words | 1,500–2,500 words |
| Pillar page / comprehensive guide | 2,500 words | 3,000–5,000 words |
| Service page | 800 words | 1,200–2,000 words |
| Case study | 500 words | 800–1,200 words |
| FAQ answer | 100 words per answer | 150–250 words per answer |
| Definition/glossary entry | 200 words | 300–500 words |
| Location page | 600 words | 800–1,500 words |

**Note:** Length is a proxy for completeness, not a goal in itself. A 1,200-word post that fully answers a specific question is better than a 2,500-word post with padding.

---

## Structural Requirements

Every blog post and service page must include:

- [ ] H1 with primary keyword
- [ ] Introduction (2–3 paragraphs) that establishes topic relevance and previews the content
- [ ] Logical heading structure (H2s → H3s)
- [ ] At least one data point, statistic, or evidence-based claim
- [ ] At least one practical, actionable section (how-to, checklist, example)
- [ ] Internal links (minimum 3)
- [ ] A conclusion or summary section
- [ ] A call to action (CTA) — relevant to the content topic

---

## Writing Style Requirements

- **Australian English** throughout (see Tone of Voice document)
- **Short paragraphs** — 2–3 sentences maximum in blog content
- **Active voice** — avoid passive constructions
- **No filler sentences** — every sentence must add value
- **No AI-sounding phrases** — avoid "It's worth noting," "Importantly," "In today's landscape," "As we delve into"
- **Correct industry terminology** — use the precise security industry terms, not approximate consumer language
- **Consistent formatting** — use bullet lists for scannable items, numbered lists for sequential steps, tables for comparisons

---

## Freshness Requirements

| Content Type | Review/Update Frequency |
|---|---|
| Algorithm-related content (SEO, AI) | Every 6 months |
| Statistics and data-driven content | Annually (or when new data is available) |
| Regulatory/compliance content | Within 30 days of any regulatory change |
| News and trends content | As needed — no scheduled review |
| Evergreen guides | Annually |
| Service pages | Annually (or when service offering changes) |

All content must display:
- Original publication date
- "Last updated" date when updated after publication

---

## Content Prohibited Practices

The following are strictly prohibited on SecurityBlogs:

1. **Thin content** — pages with less than 300 words of meaningful content
2. **Duplicate content** — publishing the same or substantially similar content on multiple pages
3. **Keyword stuffing** — unnatural repetition of keywords that degrades readability
4. **Misleading headlines** — headlines that promise content the article doesn't deliver (clickbait)
5. **Unattributed AI-generated content** — content generated by AI without human review, fact-checking, and editing
6. **Fabricated statistics** — invented numbers, inflated results, or unverifiable claims
7. **Plagiarism** — reproducing content from other websites without attribution and permission
8. **Outdated regulatory information** — publishing incorrect information about security industry regulations or licensing

---

## Content Approval Workflow

1. **Brief** — keyword target, audience, intent, word count, and structural outline agreed
2. **Draft** — content written and self-reviewed against this checklist
3. **Editorial review** — second person reviews for accuracy, quality, and tone
4. **SEO check** — title, meta description, headings, schema, internal links verified
5. **Publish** — content goes live with correct metadata
6. **Index request** — URL submitted in Google Search Console for crawling

---

## Quality Checklist (Pre-Publication)

- [ ] Minimum word count met
- [ ] Original content (not copied or AI-generated without review)
- [ ] Specific to the security industry (not generic)
- [ ] Factually accurate — claims verified
- [ ] Heading structure correct (single H1, logical H2/H3 hierarchy)
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1, first paragraph, and at least 2 H2s
- [ ] Meta title 50–60 characters with primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description 140–158 characters with CTA
- [ ] Minimum 3 internal links with descriptive anchor text
- [ ] Images have descriptive alt text
- [ ] Schema markup applied and validated
- [ ] Author byline with bio link
- [ ] Publication date set
- [ ] CTA present and relevant
- [ ] Australian English used throughout
- [ ] No prohibited practices present
