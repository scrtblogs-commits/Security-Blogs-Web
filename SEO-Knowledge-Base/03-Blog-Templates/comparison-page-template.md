# Comparison Page Template — SecurityBlogs

## How to Use This Template

Comparison pages target high-commercial-intent keywords like "[Product A] vs [Product B]," "[Service type] comparison," or "best [security product/service] for [use case]." They are among the highest-converting content types because they target buyers who are already in the consideration stage.

### INTRODUCTION

**Opening statement (2–3 sentences):**
Acknowledge the decision the reader is trying to make. Be direct — they're here to get a definitive answer, not to read about the importance of making good decisions.

*Example: "Choosing between IP CCTV and traditional analogue CCTV for a commercial property comes down to four factors: budget, future scalability, remote access requirements, and your existing infrastructure. This comparison covers all four — with a clear recommendation at the end."*

**Brief overview of both options (2–3 sentences each):**
One paragraph on Option A, one on Option B. Just enough context for readers who are less familiar with one or both.

**Bottom line up front (1–2 sentences):**
Give your recommendation immediately — readers who want the quick answer should be able to find it. You can caveat it ("the right choice depends on X"), but don't hide it.

*Total intro: 200–300 words*

## COMPARISON PAGE-SPECIFIC RULES

1. **Be genuinely helpful and honest** — do not bias comparisons toward a preferred outcome; readers trust comparison pages because they expect objectivity
2. **Give a clear recommendation** — readers want a decision, not a "it depends on everything" non-answer
3. **Include a comparison table** — the most scannable element; many readers go straight to it
4. **Update regularly** — products, services, and prices change; comparison pages become outdated quickly
5. **Link to official sources** — link to the official website, documentation, or pricing page for each option being compared
6. **Specify the audience** — always be clear about which type of security business this comparison is written for
